  • IUDs are highly effective contraceptives with a failure rate comparable to permanent sterilization
  • While the overall risk of pregnancy with an IUD is less than 1 percent, the risk of IUD failure resulting in pregnancy is the highest within the first year after IUD insertion and ranges from 0.2 to 0.8 percent
  • Risk factors for IUD failure include :
    • young age (presumably secondary to increased fertility),
    • IUD malposition, and
    • prior IUD expulsion (even if the current IUD is in the correct location)
    • The risk of pregnancy also varies by IUD type
    • Uterine position and size are not associated with increased risk of pregnancy
  • Although rare, a woman may become pregnant with an intrauterine device (IUD) in place.
  • An ultrasound should be performed to determine whether the pregnancy is intrauterine or extrauterine, since most pregnancies in this setting are ectopic.
  • If the pregnancy and IUD are intrauterine, the IUD should be removed because the risk of pregnancy complications is higher if it is left in place than if it is removed.
  • Ectopic Pregnancy
    • Although the absolute risk of ectopic pregnancy is lower for women using an IUD compared with noncontracepting women or women using other reversible contraceptives
    • IUD users are at a higher risk for ectopic pregnancy should a pregnancy occur with an IUD in situ
    • Women with an ectopic pregnancy and an IUD in place undergo ultrasound assessment of IUD location and whatever evaluation is needed for the ectopic pregnancy.
      • If the IUD needs to be removed as part of the ectopic diagnosis, such as for uterine aspiration, the patient can have a new IUD inserted during or after the ectopic management.
      • Women whose IUD is found to be malpositioned should have it removed, and a new one inserted as desired.
      • Women with a normally located IUD can leave the IUD in place despite the contraceptive failure or elect a different IUD or contraceptive method, as desired
  • Risks
    • Intrauterine pregnancies conceived with an IUD in-situ are associated with both increased maternal and fetal risks compared with the general population.
    • Maternal risk is primarily related to infection and includes septic abortion and chorioamnionitis.
    • Fetal risk associated with intrauterine pregnancy in setting of in-situ IUD includes miscarriage, preterm birth, and abruption.
  • Management- Management of intrauterine pregnancy in the setting of an IUD depends on pregnancy intentions and IUD location.
    • Undesired pregnancy
      • For women who desire termination in setting of in situ IUD, IUD removal should be performed prior to medical termination or at the time of surgical termination. The IUD can be replaced as desired immediately after surgical termination.

  • Pregnancy continuation
    • For women interested in pregnancy continuation, we remove the IUD as early as possible if the IUD strings are visible or if the IUD in within the cervix, which is consistent with ACOG, US CDC, US FDA recommendations
    • Given increased risk of infection, we advise antibiotic prophylaxis (eg, azithromycin 500 mg orally for one dose) when IUDs are removed during pregnancy.
    • There is limited evidence to guide management for women who desire pregnancy continuation when IUD strings are not visible and the IUD is not in the cervix.
    • The most recent ACOG Committee opinion advises counseling women about risks of IUD retention but not attempting IUD removal in this situation. – Given increased maternal and fetal risks associated with continuing a pregnancy with an IUD in situ (eg, sepsis), we favor a shared decision-making model.
    • If the IUD strings are not visible and the patient desires IUD removal, it is reasonable to try IUD removal under ultrasound guidance with Bozeman uterine packing forceps, a curved Kelly forceps, IUD hook, or alligator forceps as described below.
    • Hysteroscopic IUD removal can also be attempted for women who desire to continue their pregnancy and have the IUD removed. Successful hysteroscopic IUD removal has been described and is a reasonable option for women with low lying IUDs or IUDs that are distal to an intrauterine pregnancy.
